Assessment Guide for Opioid Abuse

  • Evaluate Dosage Patterns: Review the patient's medication records for dosage amounts and frequency. Frequent or high dosages may indicate a risk of opioid abuse.
  • Monitor Usage Duration: Long-term use of opioids can increase the risk of dependence. Assess the duration for which the patient has been on opioid treatment.
  • Assess Medication Adjustments: Frequent adjustments in dosage or medication may be a sign of escalating use or dependency. Track changes over time.
  • Look for Behavioral Changes: Be attentive to any behavioral changes in the patient, such as increased requests for medication or signs of withdrawal when dosages are missed.
  • Consider Drug Interactions: Check for interactions with other medications that could enhance the effects of opioids, potentially increasing the risk of abuse.
